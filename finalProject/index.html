A/B Testing in Marketing: Explaining the T-Test

Often in digital marketing and product development, changes to digital platforms are made like page or screen design updates. Promotional content is also developed and ran to increase business awareness or performance. It can be extremely difficult to measure the true impact of these design changes or promotional content. With any business there are many factors that contribute business performance making it difficult to isolate the impact of one change or promotion. The confounding factors can include:

  • Seasonal changes in the business
  • Multiple promotional offers at once
  • Changes in weather patterns, like blizzards that can affect retail store performance
  • Changes in brand perceptions

The experimental design of an A/B and multivariate test can be a nuanced process as it involves unintuitive statistics. There are different key components that need to be accounted for when running hypothesis tests, including:

  • Metric type, like continuous or discrete.
  • Sample Size
  • Distribution of the metric, including if it meets assumptions of the test.
  • Outliers within the data.
  • Summary statistics like variance of the key metric.
